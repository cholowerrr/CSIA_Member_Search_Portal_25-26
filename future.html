Future Activities

Here are the upcoming and planned initiatives of the Cybersecurity Intelligence Alliance (CSIA). These activities aim to strengthen cybersecurity awareness, provide hands-on learning, and build a strong community of cyber defenders. Note: These activities are subject to change â€” more events may be added or adjusted depending on time and circumstances.

Flag Wars: CTF Competition

To organize a competitive CTF event during SOC Days to enhance students' cybersecurity skills, encourage teamwork, and deepen their understanding of real-world security challenges.

#HAUisSecure: Cyber Awareness Campaign

To educate the HAU community on essential cybersecurity practices by posting daily pubmats focused on 2FA, safe public Wi-Fi use, avoiding suspicious links, and the risks of using cracked software.

Google Workspace Through Cybersecurity Issues

In partnership with GDG on Campus - HAU, this event provides faculty with technical knowledge to securely use Google Workspace in the academic environment.

CyberSide Up (Monthly Newsletter)

A monthly cybersecurity newsletter focusing on topics like MFA, incident response, phishing awareness, and malware trends.

CyberSecure Gens: 4th Regional Cyber Security Conference & CTF

To build knowledge and strengthen cyber defenders across all ages through a major regional conference and competitive CTF event.

ITN Review Skills Session

To help second-year students develop practical networking skills in configuring routers, switches, and devices.

ITN Review Session

A review session that reinforces Introduction to Networking concepts in preparation for preliminary exams.

Public Release of CTF Website

To officially launch the CSIA CTF website, providing access to interactive challenges that foster skill growth.

A Day with the Foxes

Promote awareness of the BS Cybersecurity program by showcasing ethical hacking training, facilities, and academic resources.

Rooted Together: A Tree Planting Activity

To advocate for environmentally and socially responsible actions that contribute to sustainable practices.

Deep Dived: Unpacking the Cyber Threat Landscape

To introduce students to advanced cybersecurity concepts like malware analysis, sandboxing, reverse engineering, and penetration testing.
